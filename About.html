Introduction to Number Systems

Number systems serve as the foundation of mathematical expressions, enabling efficient representation and manipulation of numerical values. With various number systems in use, including binary, decimal, hexadecimal, and octal, converting between these bases is crucial in computing, programming, and scientific applications. This comprehensive guide provides step-by-step instructions on converting numbers between different bases, covering:

  • Binary to Decimal: Calculate powers of 2.
  • Decimal to Binary: Divide by 2 and record remainders.
  1. 1010 (binary) = 12^3 + 02^2 + 12^1 + 02^0 = 10 (decimal)
  2. 10 (decimal) = 1010 (binary)
  • Octal to Decimal: Calculate powers of 8.
  • Decimal to Octal: Divide by 8 and record remainders.
  1. 29 (decimal) = 35 (octal)
  2. 12 (octal) = 18^1 + 28^0 = 10 (decimal)
  • Hexadecimal to Decimal: Calculate powers of 16.
  • Decimal to Hexadecimal: Divide by 16 and record remainders.
  1. 1A (hexadecimal) = 116^1 + 1016^0 = 26 (decimal)
  2. 255 (decimal) = FF (hexadecimal)
